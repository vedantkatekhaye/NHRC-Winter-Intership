**ABOUT INTERNSHIP**

The NHRC Winter Internship (also called Winter Internship Programme – WIP) is a four-week, in-person internship focused on understanding human rights law, policy, governance, and advocacy in practice. It brings together students from across India to learn directly from NHRC officials and subject-matter experts about how human rights are protected and promoted at the national level.

I have been shortlisted and selected among the 80 candidates out of 1485 applicants nationwide for the Winter Internship Programme (WIP) 2025 at the National Human Rights Commission (NHRC).

**DURATION**: 4 weeks - 15th December to 9th January.

**MODE**: Offline / on-site at the NHRC offices in New Delhi.

**WHAT DID I DO DURING THE INTERNSHIP:**

Classroom sessions and lectures on human rights issues.

Interactions with NHRC Chairperson, Members, and senior officials.

Group research projects and book reviews.

Field visits to institutions such as police stations, jails, NGOs and other commissions.

Film screenings and discussions on human rights themes.

**ACHIEVEMENTS:**

During the internship, I Worked in a 10-member team on research, analysis, and presentation and contributed to a group research project titled “Rights vs Realities: Deconstructing Consumer Rights and Duties in the Digital Era,” which secured 3rd position among all presentations.
